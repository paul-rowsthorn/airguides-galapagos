With Airguides

An island unlike anywhere on Earth

The Galápagos remain one of the last places on the planet where wildlife simply doesn't run from you. Where you can sit beside a sea lion, lock eyes with a giant tortoise, and photograph an albatross courtship dance from two metres away. This itinerary takes you to the stops where those moments happen most powerfully.

The Itinerary
San Cristóbal Island
Stop 01

León Dormido — Kicker Rock

The money shot. Sheer volcanic spires rising 148m from the Pacific, best seen at sunrise when orange-pink light reflects off still water. Snorkel the narrow channel between the two rocks — hammerhead sharks, sea turtles, and Galápagos sharks patrol the passage.

Stop 02

Cerro Brujo — Wizard Hill Beach

White coral sand contrasted against black lava rock, with electric orange and red Sally Lightfoot crabs scattered across the dark surface. The colour contrast is extraordinary. One of the most visually striking beaches in the archipelago, and almost completely deserted.

Stop 03

Punta Pitt — Three Boobies

The only place on Earth where all three booby species nest side by side — blue-footed, red-footed, and Nazca. The blue-footed booby's absurd courtship dance (lifting those improbable turquoise feet one at a time) is one of the most reliably entertaining wildlife moments in the Galápagos.

Floreana Island
Stop 04

Punta Cormorant — Flamingos

Bright pink flamingos wading in a shallow lagoon backed by an olive-green sand beach — a combination you won't see anywhere else. The beach gets its distinctive colour from olivine crystals mixed in the sand. Sea turtles lay eggs in the dunes beyond the lagoon at night.

Stop 05

Post Office Bay — The Barrel

A 200-year-old wooden barrel used by whalers as a message-passing system still stands here — and still works. Visitors leave postcards with no stamp, hoping a passing stranger heading to that destination will hand-deliver them. Some postcards have been delivered years later. Some never arrive.

Historic landmark · Shareable story · Quirky content gold

Española Island
Stop 06

Punta Suarez — Waved Albatross

Española is the only place in the world where the waved albatross nests. With a 2.4-metre wingspan, these are the largest birds in the Galápagos — and during breeding season they perform an elaborate courtship dance: bowing, sky-pointing, bill-circling, honking. The 2022 Galápagos Conservation Trust photo competition winner was an albatross shot from right here.

Stop 07

Gardner Bay — Sea Lions

One of the most beautiful beaches in the world, and one of the most unusual. The white sand at Gardner Bay is shared with a colony of Galápagos sea lions who have no instinct to avoid humans. Pups will approach you out of curiosity. Mothers sleep centimetres from your feet. Juveniles play alongside snorkellers in the crystal shallows. Also — Christmas iguanas with vivid red and green breeding colours.

Santa Cruz Island
Stop 08

Santa Cruz Highlands — Giant Tortoises

The rolling green highlands are home to free-roaming giant tortoises — some over 100 years old and weighing 250kg. In the morning mist, ancient tortoises move through the grasslands in a scene that has barely changed in a million years. Guests walk among them at eye level, with nothing between you and a creature that was alive before your grandparents were born.

Giant tortoises · Morning mist · Free-roaming wildlife

Stop 09

Charles Darwin Research Station — Lonesome George

Lonesome George was the last Pinta Island tortoise. He died in 2012, the end of an entire species, and is now preserved here. The station also runs active conservation breeding programmes that have brought several tortoise species back from the brink. Part scientific facility, part living monument to what's at stake.

Bartolomé & Santiago
Stop 10

Bartolomé — Pinnacle Rock

The most photographed view in the Galápagos, and for good reason. A 374-step boardwalk leads to a panorama that stops conversation entirely — Pinnacle Rock in the foreground, the turquoise bay below, neighbouring Santiago behind. Galápagos penguins nest at the base of the rock — tropical penguins in warm water, an impossible combination.

Stop 11

Sombrero Chino — Chinese Hat Islet

A perfectly cone-shaped volcanic island visible from the water — its silhouette looks exactly like a traditional Chinese straw hat. Snorkel the crystal-clear channel alongside Galápagos penguins, white-tipped reef sharks, and sea turtles. One of the most serene and photogenic anchorages in the archipelago.

North Seymour Island
Stop 12

North Seymour — Frigatebirds

The male magnificent frigatebird inflates a brilliant scarlet throat pouch the size of a football during breeding season — one of the most bizarre and spectacular displays in the natural world. Nothing else looks like this anywhere on Earth. Birds display within metres of guests on the trail. Blue-footed boobies perform their absurd foot-shuffle courtship dance alongside.
